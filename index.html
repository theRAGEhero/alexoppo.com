About me

I consider myself an inventor. I’ve created an open hardware smart back panel to prevent back pain, and I thrive on sharing knowledge openly.

Writing has been a lifelong passion—my first blog hosted Italian micro-novels, and storytelling continues to connect my interests in technology, history, and civic life.

Between tailoring backpacks, studying Information Technology and History, and working as a systems administrator, I found a calling in weaving together craft, research, and community.

Read my thesis, Blockchain: decentralization of money, power and the state, or browse my long-running blog 9minuti.it.

I’m fascinated by the potential of technology to democratize access and participation. That’s why I founded democracyroutes.com and host conversations with researchers, politicians, and civic technologists. The question that drives me: how can we empower more people to shape the public life they share?

When I’m not building platforms or writing, you’ll find me experimenting with new backpack patterns, chatting with university professors, or sketching the next open project.
